About UTC Time

What Is UTC Time?

UTC (Coordinated Universal Time) is the primary global time standard used to regulate clocks and timekeeping worldwide. Unlike local time zones, UTC does not observe daylight saving time and remains constant throughout the year.

Why Is UTC Used Worldwide?

UTC provides a single, consistent reference time that avoids confusion caused by time zone differences. It is essential for systems and people that operate across multiple countries.

  • Standard reference for servers, APIs, and databases
  • Used in aviation, navigation, space, and military operations
  • Prevents daylight saving time errors
  • Ensures accurate logging and scheduling

UTC vs Local Time

Local time depends on geographic location and daylight saving rules, while UTC remains the same everywhere. Many regions calculate their local time as an offset from UTC (for example, UTC+5:30).
